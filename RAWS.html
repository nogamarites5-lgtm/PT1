The Ant and the Grasshopper

A Tale of Balance

In a sunlit meadow lived Pip the ant and Zephyr the grasshopper. Pip was Diligient —from dawn till dusk, he and his colony gathered seeds, grains, and fruit, storing them in their underground nest. He knew summer wouldn't last, and they needed food for winter.

Zephyr, though, was a carefree musician. He hopped from grass to flower, playing his leaf guitar and singing songs of joy. When he saw Pip toiling with heavy loads, he'd call out, "Why work so hard? The sun's shining—come play with me!"

Pip would smile and reply, "Winter's coming. We must prepare, or we'll go hungry." Zephyr just laughed, saying winter was far away, and went back to his music.

As summer faded, the days grew short and cold and the winter came. Pip and the ants worked faster, filling their nest with food. Zephyr kept putting off work, telling himself he'd start "tomorrow." But soon, frost covered the meadow. All food was buried, and Zephyr shivered, hungry and alone. He remembered Pip's words and felt regret —he'd played all summer and had nothing left.

Weak and cold, Zephyr went to the ants' nest. "Pip, I'm hungry(growling belly) and have nowhere to go," he whispered. "I was foolish not to listen."
